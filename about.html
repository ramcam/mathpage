Welcome to $ tiny \ math $, a site dedicated to explaining math in an accessible way, one tiny step at a time. Here is were I write about topics I encountered during my math studies in a style which works better for me. The focus is placed on observating math closely, asking questions and finding examples. Once some intuitions are in place, it is easier to sketch a proof that will confirm or challenge them. I hope this approach works for you as well!

Although the prerequisites vary depending on the topic, the content in general should be suitable for anyone with basic background in math. I have undergraduate math students especially in mind when writing since I was one of them not so long ago.

$ tiny \ math $ grew out (and still does) from a series of oral math lessons given while jogging through Oslo. My jogging companion and recipient of the lessons is Salas, my partner. With no pencil and paper or any other visual means at hand, the oral nature of the lessons forced me to build up concepts slowly, relying mostly on mental images and boiling down topics to the most essential intuitions. Salas natural talent to ask just the right question contributed to gradually shape the lessons. This website is dedicated to her.
