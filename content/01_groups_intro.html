What is a group?

Informally, a group is an algebraic structure where operations can always be undone. Take a binary operation $✿$ and a set $G$ closed respect to $✿$. Any two elements &g, h \in G$ can be combined to produce a third like so: \[ g✿h = k. \] You may think of this as something we did to $h$ on its right side. Since we said every operation in our group may be undone, it should be possible to write \[ g^{-1}✿k = g^{-1}✿g✿h = h \] to obtain the original $h$. In order for this to work properly, we need that $g^{-1}✿g$ equals something that leaves $h$ untoched. We call this the identity element $e$. Then \[ g^{-1}✿k = g^{-1}✿g✿h = e✿h = h. \] Why is this interesting? A couple of reasons: You probably heard that wherever there is symmetry, there is a group. Also, the simplest equation you can imagine, &ax=b& requires you to use groups and nothing more in order to solve it. (Add axiomatic definition.)
